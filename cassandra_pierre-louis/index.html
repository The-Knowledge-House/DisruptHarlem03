What is ColourWay?

Simply put, ColourWay is a way to share your travel experiences with people just like you, or nothing like you. ColourWay lets you tell us who you are, and who you identify as, and tailors your suggestions, and ultimately your travel experience, with those things in mind.

ColourWay gives you the tools necessary to have a safer more inclusive travel experience through travel deals and suggestions, based on the budget you provide us, the ability to form travel groups based on similarities or interests, in-app navigation loaded with safety features and alerts,especially for women, and peer reviews from people just like you!


Inclusion. Access. Connection.

ColourWay is committed to ensuring that people of color are equipped to have the travel experience they've always wanted, regardless of their skin color or ethnic background.

 Our key features make travel experiences easier and safer to plan, book, and enjoy. From user reviews to photo/video editing, to itinerary and budget planning, ColourWay has got it covered!

Cassandra L. Pierre-Louis

(CEO & Founder)


Cassandra Pierre-Louis is the CEO & Founder of ColourWay, a community of people who come together to share travel experiences and share tip and reviews, amongst other things, on a platform that promotes inclusion in relation to travel

An avid traveler, Cassandra has always approached her travel planning with a bit of trepidation. She has always had concerns about traveling as a woman, but more specifically as a black woman. Her hopes are that this app provides not only a forum and platform for sharing photos, tips, and deals, but that it functions as a safe space for those who don't necessarily feel safe when traveling because of the color of their skin and how they identify.

A native of the Bronx, Cassandra holds a BS in Business Administration: Finance from Mercy College. Prior to starting ColourWay, Cassandra functioned both as Social Media Director and, later, Research Chair for BelTIFi Inc., a non-profit organization based in New York that serves young Haitian-American women across New York State in the areas of self-care, mentor-ship, and professional development. Her future plans also include spreading technology education and encouraging start-up development in Haiti to promote economic independence through financial literacy programs and curriculum.
